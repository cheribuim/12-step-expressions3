Step 1

   We admitted we were powerless over alcohol—that our lives had become unmanageable.

Do you feel like the waves of your addiction and the pressure to quit are crashing in on you? Do you feel like the barn and the winds of life and addiction are blowing you over? Because the barn is built stationary and refuses to move from its foundation, it is destroyed! If, as an addict, we would become more like the waves and be willing to roll and be shapeless and willing to be changed, destruction wouldn't affect us.



How does one know when their lives have become unmanageable ?

Have you lost jobs, because you couldn't get up after days on binges? Have you had DCS called in to check on your kid’s cause of your addiction? Has your addiction landed you in jail more than once? Have you lost marriages or spouses cause you wouldn't quit your drug of choice? Have you become violent or aggressive towards family and friends? What about seclusion? Have you isolated yourself from everyone, from embarrassment or hoping that you can hide your problems? These are all examples and questions that can help you understand if your life is unmanageable! But don't despair there is hope!



How does someone admit powerlessness and to who?

Sometimes the only way one can admit being powerless is to be taken to a low level of having things they really love taken or run away from them. Periods of jail time or in rehabs give us time to stop and reflect. Others will see it from deaths around them. The first person that you have to admit it to is yourself; because until you realize it, nothing anyone tries to do to help you will make a difference. You've heard the old saying, “you can lead a horse to water but you can’t make them drink.” There's no truer saying to an addict.

It's hard to admit defeat or powerlessness over something that many of us, as addicts, cannot even recognize. We often think that everyone else is the problem.

As we move through these 12 steps, stop and think about you and no one else around you! Know that you are not alone! All recovering addicts, worldwide welcome you. One day at a time, one step in a focused and sober mind. Each day will seem strange at first, keep in mind your addiction didn't happen overnight and your recovery won't happen overnight either, but if you will give your recovery as much attention as you did your addiction, you will succeed.

GOD grant me the serenity to accept the things I cannot change, the courage to change the things I can and the wisdom to know the difference.

One day at a time!

The contents of this web site are not only from the AA 12 Steps but from Recovering addicts , past and present. Who better to guide you through this journey than the ones that have walked it before you and made it.
